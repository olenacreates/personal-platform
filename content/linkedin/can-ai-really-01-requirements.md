I'm starting a new experiment.

Over the next few months, I'll be building my personal website with AI.

Not because I'm looking for shortcuts.
Because I'm curious.

I want to know what building software with AI actually looks like —
beyond the demos and the headlines.

Can AI write good requirements?
Design an architecture?
Review documentation?
Generate code?
Find real bugs?

I'll document the whole process here — what works, what doesn't,
and what surprises me along the way.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Can AI Really…? #1
Can AI write good product requirements?

🎯 What I expected
A fast drafter: describe my idea, get back a neat requirements document.

🧪 What I actually did
The opposite. I didn't ask AI to write requirements — I ran a structured
discovery where AI was the facilitator, not the author. One question at a time.
It challenged my assumptions, surfaced contradictions I'd glossed over, and made
me defend my scope — then summarized what we'd agreed and flagged what was still
missing.

😯 The most interesting discovery
The value wasn't AI writing requirements. It was AI interrogating them.
It caught places where I'd said one thing here and something different there.
It refused to let vague goals pass. And a self-review at the end found real gaps
before I locked anything in.
Left alone to "just write the requirements," it would have produced something
plausible — and shallow. Directed to ask, it made my thinking sharper.

✅ Verdict: Surprisingly, yes — but not the way I expected.
AI didn't write my requirements. It helped me think clearly enough to write them
myself. The result is a product definition I actually trust — because I earned it
through the questions, not around them.
